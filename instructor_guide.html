This guide walks you through how to use the Herely system to manage your course, take attendance, and review records.

To create a new course, simply click on the "+ Add New Course" button from the Dashboard. You'll be asked for basic course info, like title and semester of the course.

After submitting, you'll see a new course in your dashboard. Before taking attendance, you will need to add students/participants to your course roster. To manage the roster, click "Manage Course" in the course card. Navigate to "Manage Roster" to:

  • Add class participants manually by name and email
  • Upload a CSV file for bulk roster creation
  • Edit or remove class participants at any time

Usage recommendation: use CSV upload to create your class participant roster. You can download a CSV roster from Canvas ("New Analytics -> "Reports").

Student/participant attendance is linked to meetings that you create. A meeting specifies the date, start time, and duration of an attendance call. If you click "Start Meeting," a meeting will start immediately and use defaults for sign-in duration. If you click "Schedule Meeting," you will be able to schedule future meetings — use recurring meetings to schedule the entire course in one go.

Once you start a meeting, participants will be able to sign in at the course sign-in page — you will need to share the course link or QR code with the students. The link and QR code are in the course card located in the Dashboard.

False attendance is managed by limited sign-in times and the written record of their sign-in. We believe that participants should be trusted — but you also have a record to inspect and can follow up with participants as necessary.

Usage recommendation: in a pinch, just click "Start Meeting" using your phone at the time you want to actually take attendance. It will default to the current time and ten minute attendance duration.

From the dashboard, click "View Attendance Records" for any course. You can:

  • See which students/participants signed in for each meeting
  • Examine attendance rates
  • Zoom in on a sign-in to see their written sign-in
  • Export attendance records to CSV
  • Manually mark students/participants present/absent/excused if needed
  • Edit or delete meetings

Usage recommendation: regularly review attendance and sort the table by percent absence — students/participants who miss many classes may be in distress.
